Ananta is a Sanskrit term which means 'endless' or 'limitless', also means 'eternal' or 'infinity',[1] in other words, it also means infinitude or an unending expansion or without limit. It is one of the many names of Lord Vishnu.[2] Ananta is the Shesha-naga, the celestial snake, on which Lord Vishnu reclines.
